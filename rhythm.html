Section 1 · Definition

"An HRBP Operating Rhythm is the structured cadence of activities — recurring and triggered — that enables an HR Business Partner to stay proactively embedded in the business rather than responding reactively to events after they occur."

Why a Rhythm Exists

Not a to-do list.
A governance mechanism.

Each activity has a named owner, a defined output, and a stated purpose tied to a business outcome. Without a defined rhythm, the HRBP role defaults to firefighting — processing transactions and resolving immediate issues rather than building the workforce capability the business needs.

The Problem It Solves
Reactive vs. Proactive
+
Without a defined rhythm, urgent operational tasks crowd out strategic ones. The HRBP becomes a responder to whatever arrives in the inbox — grievances, payroll queries, ad-hoc requests — rather than the architect of a BU workforce that meets business targets.
What the Rhythm Governs
Five Time Horizons
+
Daily: operational integrity. Weekly: focus and triage. Monthly: analytical depth and compliance. Quarterly: strategic cycles — PMS, talent calibration, succession. Annual: governance, people plan, and operating rhythm review itself.
For Arvind HRBPs Specifically
Balancing Two Demands
+
Managing multi-location workforce across Wovens, Knits, and Garmenting means the HRBP must balance strategic interventions (succession, talent health, org effectiveness) with operational integrity (compliance, grievance resolution, payroll accuracy). This document maps that balance.
The Discipline Standard
Rhythm Is Identity
+
An HRBP who works to a rhythm has decided to be proactive. One without a rhythm is defined by whatever landed in their inbox this morning. The difference between a transactional HR generalist and a strategic HRBP is not capability — it is discipline.
Section 2 · Daily Operating Rhythm

Nine activities.
Every working day.

How many of these nine activities are you doing today — intentionally, not reactively? The ones you skip are the ones that will surface as problems in 30 days.

01 Morning Attendance & Absenteeism Review ›
Review attendance data for the HRBP's BU. Identify same-day absenteeism above threshold. Flag production-impacting absences to the HR head / Functional Head.
02 HR Helpdesk Queue Triage ›
Review and route or resolve overnight employee queries on payroll, leave balances, transfer requests, and grievances. All queries acknowledged within 2 hours of business start.
03 Manager Pulse Connect — 1 Per Day ›
Brief 15-minute check-in with one BU manager per day to stay current on team health, emerging performance concerns, or hiring pressures. Rotated across all managers in a 4-week cycle.
04 Pending Approval Review ›
Ensure no approval is pending for more than 24 hours — offers, confirmations, transfers. Identify stuck approvals and escalate to COE as warranted. Maintain zero-aging on critical approvals.
05 Talent Acquisition Pipeline Check ›
Review open requisition status for the BU. Confirm interview schedules with hiring managers. Flag any role stalled beyond SLA (30 days). Coordinate with TA team on shortlists or offer negotiations.
06 Employee Floor Walk or Informal Connect ›
Brief shopfloor or office walkthrough to pick up informal pulse — visible signs of disengagement, informal grievances, or morale signals that will not appear in formal data for weeks. Document observations in daily log.
07 Escalation & Grievance Resolution Follow-up ›
Review any open grievances or escalations from previous 48 hours. Confirm resolution path, owner, and deadline. Any grievance older than 3 working days without an owner is an escalation trigger.
08 Data Hygiene & HRMS Update ›
Ensure all day's people movements — joining, exits, transfers, promotions — are reflected in HRMS. Data accuracy by end of day is non-negotiable.
09 End-of-Day Done / Doing / Stuck Note ›
Three-bullet status note: one item completed, one in progress, one blocked. Sent daily to HR Head or manager. Creates accountability trail and surfaces blockers before they become escalations.
📋
Daily log discipline: Every floor walk observation, every manager connect insight, and every escalation decision must be documented. Pattern recognition across weeks is only possible if daily notes exist. The log is the HRBP's institutional memory.
Section 3 · Weekly Operating Rhythm

One focus area
per day. Every week.

Each working day carries a distinct focus area, ensuring that every critical HRBP function receives dedicated attention at least once per week and does not get displaced by daily firefighting.

Monday
Focus Area
Business Alignment & Data Refresh
Key Activities
Review people dashboard updated over the weekend: attrition MTD, open roles, absenteeism trend, PMS completion status. Brief the HR head on any weekend developments. Set the week's 3 priority people items.
Output
Weekly HRBP priority list set — three clearly named people items the HRBP will move forward this week, shared with the HR Head by 10 AM.
Tuesday
Focus Area
Talent Acquisition & Hiring Pipeline
Key Activities
Recruitment pipeline review: progress on all open requisitions, interview feedback received, offers pending, rejections requiring re-sourcing. Coordinate with TA on any stalled roles. Raise new requisitions if approved headcount is pending.
Output
Recruitment status update; any stalled role formally flagged with reason; new requisitions raised in ATS; TA coordination completed.
Wednesday
Focus Area
Manager Capability & Performance
Key Activities
Structured manager check-ins (2 per week). Focus on: underperformance conversations in progress, PMS completion gaps, feedback quality, and team health signals. Mid-week is best for substantive conversations — managers are neither in week-start mode nor week-close mode.
Output
HRBP conversation log updated; any manager coaching action documented; PIP initiations progressed; PMS completion gaps escalated where needed.
Thursday
Focus Area
Employee Engagement & Retention
Key Activities
Review exit interviews submitted in past 7 days; identify common themes; flag any at-risk high performer for pre-emptive retention conversation. Run scheduled stay interviews with identified at-risk employees.
Output
Exit theme note drafted; at-risk employee list updated; stay interview outputs documented and shared with HR Head if any flight risk identified.
Friday
Focus Area
Governance, Compliance & Planning
Key Activities
Review the week's escalations, compliance deadlines for the coming week, and any statutory obligations due. Prepare the weekly status note for HR Head covering the week's progress against the Monday priority list.
Output
Weekly status note sent to HR Head; action item list with owners and deadlines confirmed; compliance calendar for the following week reviewed and flagged.
Section 4 · Monthly Operating Rhythm

Twelve activities.
Structured by week of month.

Distributed across four weeks to prevent bunching at month-end. Each activity has a stakeholder, an expected output, and a frequency note that specifies when it becomes non-negotiable.

BU-level report covering actual headcount vs. approved plan, MTD attrition, joinings, exits, and open roles by grade. Shared by Day 5 of the month. Forms the basis of all people conversations for the month.
TA LeadManpower requisition review — reconcile open requisitions against approved manpower plan; close roles open beyond SLA or re-justify to business.Every month
All open requisitions reconciled against approved manpower plan. Roles open beyond SLA either closed or formally re-justified. ATS updated. Prevents headcount drift and ensures hiring stays within plan.
HR Ops + HRBPNew joiner onboarding completion check (Day 30 cohort) — confirm statutory enrollment, induction sign-off, probation KRA setting, and manager introduction.Every month
Confirmation that Day 30 joiners have completed: statutory enrollment (PF, ESI), induction sign-off, probation KRA setting, and manager introduction. Any incomplete items flagged to HR Ops with 48-hour correction deadline.
HR Head + ManagersPMS status review — completion rates for all E and above grade employees: KRA submission, mid-year review, or annual appraisal. Non-compliant managers flagged.Every month during PMS cycle
PMS completion rates for all E and above grade employees documented. Non-compliant managers formally flagged to HR Head with a correction deadline. Completion rate tracked as an HRBP KPI.
Payroll TeamPayroll input validation — confirm all HRBP-owned inputs (new joins, exits, promotions, performance-linked variable pay) submitted before payroll cut-off date.Every month — zero tolerance for delay
All HRBP-owned payroll inputs confirmed submitted to the payroll team by cut-off date. Payroll errors trace back to late HRBP inputs — zero-tolerance policy. Confirmation sent to payroll team in writing.
HR HeadManager 1:1 completion tracking — confirm all scheduled manager check-ins for the month completed; any missed manager rescheduled within the same month.Every month
Completion rate for all manager check-ins tracked and reported to HR Head. Any manager missed in the cycle rescheduled within the same month. Completion rate tracked as an HRBP KPI.
HRBPMonthly attrition deep-dive and retention analysis — voluntary vs. involuntary, by grade, tenure, department, and reason code. Propose targeted retention interventions for at-risk cohorts.Every month; BU > 5% requires written action plan
Attrition analysis: voluntary vs. involuntary, by grade, tenure, department, and reason code. Patterns identified. Targeted retention interventions proposed for at-risk cohorts. Any BU exceeding 5% monthly attrition requires a written action plan.
CHRO / HR HeadTalent health dashboard review — bench strength, critical role gaps, HiPo attrition YTD, succession bench coverage, IDP completion. Presented at monthly HR Head 1:1.Every month
BU talent health dashboard updated: succession bench coverage, HiPo attrition YTD, critical role vacancy age, IDP completion rate. Presented to HR Head in monthly 1:1. Inputs to quarterly 9-Box and succession planning cycle.
HRBPExit interview theme analysis — aggregate exit data for the month; identify top 3 attrition reasons; themes repeating 3 consecutive months trigger formal retention initiative.Every month
Aggregated exit interview data for the month. Top 3 attrition reasons identified. Themes that repeat for 3 consecutive months trigger a formal retention initiative with CHRO involvement.
E+ grade sampleEngagement pulse check — 5-question digital pulse survey or 2 stay interviews per week. Gauge engagement momentum between annual surveys.Monthly (months without quarterly survey)
5-question digital pulse survey or 2 stay interviews per week. Monthly pulse applies in months without quarterly survey; stay interviews run year-round. Insights fed into monthly HR Head 1:1.
HR Compliance + PayrollCompliance check — PF ECR by 15th, ESI challan by 21st, all FFS settlements for exited employees processed within 30-day window.Every month — any default is immediate escalation
Statutory filings confirmed: PF ECR by 15th, ESI challan by 21st. All FFS (full and final settlements) for exited employees processed within 30-day window. Any default is an immediate escalation to HR Head — creates statutory risk for Arvind.
HR Head + CHROMonthly HR governance report and HR Head 1:1 — HRBP presents people dashboard, attrition analysis, talent update, and compliance status. One decision required before meeting ends.Every month — CHRO version by last working day
Monthly people dashboard, attrition analysis, talent update, and compliance status presented to HR Head. One decision committed before the meeting ends. CHRO version shared by last working day of the month.
Section 5 · Quarterly Operating Rhythm

Strategic cycles that
drive the full year.

Every quarterly activity has a hard deadline within the quarter and a named output that feeds the next cycle. These are not optional — they are the engine of the HRBP operating model.

Mar–Apr · Deadline: April 30
OKR Setting — E and Above Grade
OKRs set for the year in alignment with business objectives. Team Member + Manager. Concurrent with Cycle Closure of the prior year. Foundation of the full PMS cycle.
Signed OKRs in HRMS for 100% of E and above grade population. Completion rate tracked and reported to CHRO. Prior-year Cycle Closure formally locked by April 30.
April 1–15
Annual People Plan & Headcount Finalisation
HRBP presents BU-level people plan to HR Head and CHRO: approved headcount, attrition forecast, succession priorities, L&D plan, and key hiring milestones for the year.
BU People Plan document submitted to CHRO by April 15. Secures budget and headcount approvals for the year.
April – May
Apprentice / Trainee Intake Planning
New academic batch joins in April–May. HRBP coordinates with GCC on onboarding, induction, and Apprenticeship Act compliance. First formal pulse of new joiners at Day 30.
Apprentice joining register; Day 30 pulse results; any early disengagement signals flagged to HR Head.
July
Q1 Check-In — E and Above Grade
Periodic progress conversation between Team Member and Manager. Challenges and support requirements surfaced and documented. Upstream input to Mid-Year Review in Sep–Oct.
Q1 Check-In completion rate logged in HRMS. Unresolved support flags escalated to HR Head within 7 days.
July – August
First 9-Box Talent Calibration
First 9-Box of the year for all E and above grade workforce. Identify HiPos, flagging, and development interventions. Initiate IDPs for identified HiPos. Succession map for critical roles updated.
9-Box grid per BU; succession map updated; IDPs initiated for HiPos within 30 days of calibration.
August – September
Annual Engagement Survey Launch & Results
Full-cycle engagement survey across all grades. HRBP presents BU-level results to HR Head. Action plans drafted and committed by HR Head before end of quarter.
BU engagement score vs. company average; action plan shared with HR Head; CHRO consolidated view by September 30.
Sep – Oct · Deadline: October 31
Mid-Year Review — E and Above Grade
Formal half-year assessment (TM + Manager + Reviewer). Feedforward inputs for H2 planning. Performance trajectory, development needs, and H2 priorities identified.
Mid-Year Review completion rate ≥ 95% by October 31. Feedforward documented in HRMS. H2 development actions committed.
April – May (post Annual Assessment)
Annual Increment & Compensation Review
C&B COE benchmarking initiated after Annual Assessment ratings are locked. HRBP provides BU performance context, equity inputs, and retention risk flags. HRBP reviews draft increment matrix before letters are issued in May.
Increment matrix sign-off; retention risk flag for top performers before letters sent; letters issued by May 31.
January – February
Annual Succession Planning Presentation to CHRO
Full succession bench review for all critical roles. HRBP presents bench strength, development gaps, and cross-BU movement recommendations to CHRO.
Succession dashboard; critical role risk ratings; cross-BU succession moves approved by CHRO.
February – March · Deadline: March 15
Workforce Plan for Next FY — Headcount Requisitions
HRBP works with HR Head to project next year's headcount needs: new roles, backfill projections, natural attrition estimates. Submitted to CHRO for budget approval.
Draft headcount plan for next FY submitted by March 15. CHRO-approved by March 31.
January
Check-In — E and Above Grade
Periodic progress conversation between Team Member and Manager. Course correction before Annual Assessment in Mar–Apr. Flags any H2 performance concerns early.
Check-In completion rate logged in HRMS. Critical performance concerns escalated to HR Head before Annual Assessment cycle opens.
Mar–Apr · Deadline: April 30
Annual Assessment — E and Above Grade
Full-cycle evaluation (TM + Reviewer + Calibration Panel). Ratings, promotions, and development direction decided. Most consequential PMS event of the year.
Final ratings submitted to CHRO by April 30. Promotion decisions communicated. Development plans initiated within 30 days of assessment.
March · Annual
Annual HR Operating Rhythm Review
HRBP reviews effectiveness of current year's rhythm: what cadences held, what slipped, what must be redesigned. Inputs to updated Operating Rhythm document for next FY.
Updated HRBP Operating Rhythm document. Signed off by HR Head before April 1.

Section 7 · Activity Categorisation Matrix

Four quadrants.
One target state.

Every HRBP activity falls into one of four quadrants defined by Frequency (Recurring vs. Triggered) and Nature (Strategic vs. Operational). Click any quadrant to expand its activity list.

What HRBPs often miss

Most HRBPs land in the bottom-right quadrant — triggered and operational — because that is where the inbox points them. The target is top-left: recurring and strategic. Getting there requires protecting your calendar before others fill it.

Section 8 · Key Metrics an HRBP Tracks

12 metrics. Every one
with a threshold and a trigger.

Metrics without thresholds are observations. Metrics with thresholds are governance instruments. The 12 below constitute the minimum monitoring set for every HRBP at Arvind.

Review cadence: Six metrics — attrition, PMS completion, headcount variance, FFS clearance rate, succession bench coverage, and absenteeism — are reviewed at the monthly HR Head 1:1. The remaining six are reviewed at the quarterly CHRO governance pack.
Monthly · HR Head 1:1
Monthly Attrition Rate
Voluntary E and above grade exits in the month ÷ average headcount for the month, expressed as a percentage.
MonthlyHRBP
> 5%/month → written retention action plan. > 10% → CHRO escalation.
Monthly · HR Head 1:1
PMS Completion Rate
% of E and above grade employees with completed KRAs, mid-year reviews, or annual appraisals documented in HRMS (as applicable to month).
Monthly (PMS cycle)HRBP
< 95% by deadline → HR Head escalation. < 85% → formal governance failure.
Monthly · HR Head 1:1
Headcount vs. Approved Manpower Plan
Actual headcount compared to approved manpower plan per BU and grade band, expressed as variance percentage.
MonthlyHRBP
Variance > 3% → explanation to HR Head. > 5% → CHRO approval before correction.
Monthly · HR Head 1:1
Absenteeism Rate
% of scheduled production hours lost to unplanned E and above grade absenteeism; split by department and day of week.
Weekly / MonthlyHRBP
> 5% on any shift → same-day escalation. Chronic (3+ weeks > 5%) → formal counselling process.
Monthly · HR Head 1:1
Succession Bench Coverage
% of critical roles (M3 and above) with at least one identified internal successor (ready-now or 1–2 year horizon) documented in succession tracker.
QuarterlyHRBP + CHRO
< 80% → board-level talent risk. < 60% → immediate cross-BU succession planning intervention.
Monthly · HR Head 1:1
Time-to-Fill
Calendar days from requisition approval to offer acceptance for E and above grade roles; split by level and BU.
MonthlyHRBP + TA Lead
> 30 days (M1–M2) or > 45 days (M3+) → TA escalation meeting triggered.
Quarterly · CHRO Pack
Regrettable Attrition Rate
Attrition among employees rated High Performer (A-rating) or HiPo in the last 9-Box. The most strategically significant attrition metric an HRBP tracks.
QuarterlyHRBP + CHRO
> 5% of HiPo population/quarter. Any individual regrettable exit → post-mortem with HR Head within 48 hours.
Quarterly · CHRO Pack
Employee Engagement Score
BU-level engagement score from quarterly pulse or annual survey vs. enterprise average; expressed as absolute score and gap.
QuarterlyHRBP
BU < company average → mandatory action plan within 30 days. Gap > 0.5 points → CHRO flag.
Quarterly · CHRO Pack
Manager Effectiveness Score
Composite from direct-report pulse surveys, 360 feedback, and HRBP 1:1 observations; measures quality of people management in the BU.
QuarterlyHRBP
Score < 3.0/5.0 for two consecutive quarters → structured coaching referral. Persistent low scores → HR Head escalation.
Quarterly · CHRO Pack
IDP Completion Rate
% of identified HiPos and succession candidates with a current, documented, and manager-signed IDP in HRMS.
QuarterlyHRBP
< 80% among HiPos → succession quality failure. Any HiPo without IDP within 60 days of 9-Box → flagged in monthly HR review.
Quarterly · CHRO Pack
HRBP to Employee Span of Control
Ratio of HRBP coverage to E and above grade employee population; indicates whether the HRBP is operationally over-stretched.
Annual (reviewed quarterly)CHRO
Ideal: 1 HRBP per 400–500 employees. Above 700 → headcount review required.
Quarterly · CHRO Pack
HRBP Activity Split (Strategic vs. Operational)
Self-reported and manager-validated split of HRBP time across the four activity matrix quadrants; tracked against the 65/20/15/<15 target.
QuarterlyHRBP + HR Head
Triggered-Operational > 15% → rhythm review with HR Head. Recurring-Strategic < 50% → intervention required.
