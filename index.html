The Operating Model

Your world at Arvind.

The Ulrich HR Model structures Arvind's HR function into four distinct roles. Understanding where you end and where others begin is the first operating decision you will make.

HRBP · You
HR Business Partner
Embedded in the business unit. Accountable for people outcomes at the BU level. Serves management layer E1 through BM.
Strategic Partner · Medium-long term
COE · Programme Design
Centres of Excellence
Designs enterprise-wide HR frameworks, policies, and programmes (TA, L&D, C&B). Based at corporate/HQ. No BU-level decision authority.
Design & Governance · 12–24 month cycles
— Ulrich HR Model · Arvind Limited —
HOD (IR) · Plant Operations
Head of Department (IR)
Manages HR operations, IR, and statutory compliance on the ground at the plant. Serves the entire plant workforce with heaviest focus on shopfloor.
Operational · Daily & weekly cadence
GCC · Transactions
Global Capability Centre
Delivers HR transactions accurately and at scale — payroll, letters, HRIS data. Currently being built at Arvind. No discretionary authority.
Transactional · SLA-driven · Being built

The boundary rule: If it is strategic, data-driven, or advisory — it is yours. If it is transactional, compliance-driven, or shopfloor-specific — redirect it. Dependency is failure.

Role Clarity

Your role in one page.

Diagnostic interviews reveal HRBPs currently spend 70–75% of their time on out-of-scope activities. This table resets that.

What You Own
  • Workforce planning and headcount forecasting (E grade & above)
  • Performance calibration facilitation for all E+ employees
  • Succession planning for critical and senior roles
  • Talent acquisition facilitation for E grade and above
  • Employee engagement diagnosis and action planning (BU level)
  • Manager capability coaching — feedback, underperformance, team health
  • HiPo identification, 9-box calibration, individual development plans
  • Business review participation with people agenda ownership
What You Don't Touch
  • Payroll processing, salary queries, PF/ESIC administration — GCC
  • Shopfloor attendance tracking and leave management — HOD (IR)
  • Letter issuance (experience, relieving, salary) — GCC
  • Shopfloor disciplinary proceedings and domestic enquiries — HOD (IR)
  • Union negotiations and collective bargaining — HOD (IR) & IR team
  • HRIS data entry and record maintenance — GCC
  • Contract labour documentation and statutory compliance — HOD (IR)
  • Canteen, transport, and facility welfare — HOD (IR)
What Success Looks Like
  • Business first, HR secondEvery recommendation starts from a business problem — not an HR process.
  • Outcomes over activitiesMeasured by attrition, succession bench, and better leadership decisions.
  • Presence earns influenceAuthority is relational, not structural. Walk the shopfloor.
  • Dependency is failureA strong HRBP makes the plant head a better people manager.
  • Data over opinionEvery substantive conversation is backed by workforce data.

The Ramp Plan

Your first 90 days, sequenced.

Three phases, each building on the last. By Day 90, you are not a new joiner — you are an HRBP with a people agenda, a stakeholder map, and a first strategic intervention underway.

Day
1–30
Phase 01 · Orient

Orient & Observe

  • Complete BU orientation and plant floor walk with HOD (IR)
  • Map all key stakeholders — plant heads, HODs, COE leads
  • Read the complete HRBP Playbook in sequence
  • Understand the grade structure (E1 through BM)
  • Attend first business review as an observer
  • Request and review last 6-month attrition data for your BU
Day
31–60
Phase 02 · Engage

Engage & Diagnose

  • Conduct 1:1 introductions with plant head and functional HODs
  • Attend first business review with people agenda contribution
  • Pull talent health data — attrition risk, HiPo list, vacancy age
  • Identify the single largest people risk in your BU
  • Establish monthly one-on-one cadence with plant head
  • Meet COE leads — TA, L&D, C&B — and align on BU priorities
Day
61–90
Phase 03 · Own

Own & Deliver

  • Present first BU people dashboard to plant head
  • Initiate one strategic intervention (succession gap, engagement action, or PIP)
  • Establish operating rhythm — weekly check-ins, monthly reviews
  • Draft BU people agenda for the next quarter
  • Conduct first stay interviews with at-risk high performers
  • Share intelligence brief upward to CHRO on ground-level signals

The HR Corner

Because every HRBP needs
a sense of humour.

Panel 01 of 03

The HRBP at their first business review.

You have data. You have a chart. You have rehearsed your three talking points. The plant head asks one question and none of your slides cover it. This is fine. This is Tuesday. The HRBP who improvises with data still wins.

Panel 02 of 03

The 9-box calibration session.

Every manager believes their team consists entirely of Stars and High Potentials. The HRBP's job is to facilitate the conversation that produces a calibrated, data-backed distribution — diplomatically, without losing anyone's goodwill. Easiest meeting of the year. Absolutely not.

Panel 03 of 03

When the plant head calls for a payroll query.

Your phone rings. You answer expecting a talent emergency. It is a PF question. The HRBP who redirects confidently — with the right contact and a warm handoff — has just modelled exactly what this playbook is built for. Out of scope. Every time.
