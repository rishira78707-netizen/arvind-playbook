Defining the HRBP Role
at Arvind Limited

Role clarity is where HRBP effectiveness begins. This charter defines the philosophy, scope, responsibilities, and decision rights that govern how the HRBP function operates across Arvind's business units.

Section 1 · Role Foundation

Built on the Ulrich Model.
Measured by business outcomes.

The HRBP at Arvind is not a generalist HR function. It is the people-strategy engine of the business unit — embedded, accountable, and evaluated on impact rather than process completion. Three functions, one model.

Function 01
HR Business Partners
Embedded in business units. Focused on business outcomes, workforce strategy, and data-driven decision support. Physically and mentally part of the business, not a visitor.
Function 02
Centres of Excellence
Specialist programme design. Own enterprise-wide HR frameworks, policies, and capabilities. COE outputs apply to all BUs — designed centrally, deployed everywhere.
Function 03
Global Capability Centre
Transactional delivery at scale. Payroll, letters, HRIS maintenance, statutory compliance. High-volume, accuracy-critical, rule-based execution. Frees HRBPs to focus on strategy.
Operating Commitment 01 of 05

Business first,
HR second.

Every recommendation, conversation, and intervention starts from a business problem — not an HR process. The HRBP understands the BU's commercial context, cost pressures, and people risks before proposing any solution.

Operating Commitment 02 of 05

Outcomes over
activities.

The HRBP is measured by whether attrition came down, succession bench improved, and business leaders made better people decisions — not by the number of appraisals facilitated or forms processed.

Operating Commitment 03 of 05

Presence
earns influence.

Authority in this role is relational, not structural. Influence is built by attending business reviews, knowing the P&L, walking the shopfloor, and consistently bringing data-backed insight before the business leader asks for it.

Operating Commitment 04 of 05

Dependency
is failure.

A strong HRBP makes the plant head a better people manager — not a more dependent one. If a business leader cannot make a talent decision without the HRBP doing the work for them, the model is not functioning correctly.

Operating Commitment 05 of 05

Data over
opinion.

HRBPs at Arvind are expected to bring workforce data — attrition trends, performance distributions, succession bench status — to every substantive conversation. Recommendations without data carry limited weight in a manufacturing leadership forum.

1.3 · Role Scope

What the HRBP owns, influences,
and must never do.

Diagnostic interviews indicate HRBPs currently spend 70–75% of their time on activities that fall outside the scope defined below. This table is the reference for all scope decisions.

Workforce planning and headcount forecasting›
For E and above grade employees — leading annual headcount planning for the BU in partnership with the plant head and finance. Producing evidence-based workforce plans accounting for attrition forecasts, skill gaps, and business growth targets.
Performance calibration facilitation›
For all E+ grade employees — facilitating the full PMS cycle from KRA alignment through mid-year review to annual calibration. Ensuring rating distributions are equitable and data-driven. Coaching managers on structured developmental feedback.
Succession planning for critical and senior roles›
Owning succession bench mapping for all critical and senior roles (M and above). Conducting bi-annual 9-box calibration with the plant head. Ensuring every critical role has at least one identified internal successor with a documented development plan.
Talent acquisition (E and above): JD review, interviews, offer approval›
Participating in shortlisting, interviewing, and offer approval for E and above grade roles. Reviewing job descriptions for accuracy and business relevance. Managing business leader expectations on timelines and owning candidate experience.
Employee engagement diagnosis and action planning›
At BU level — analysing engagement and pulse survey data. Identifying root causes rather than surface scores. Building and owning a quarterly action plan with the HR head. Tracking progress at every monthly one-on-one.
Manager capability coaching›
Identifying people management capability gaps among BU managers. Working with the L&D COE to nominate targeted interventions. Conducting individual coaching conversations on feedback, underperformance handling, and team trust.
HiPo identification, 9-box calibration, individual development plans›
Identifying and nominating HiPos for leadership programmes, cross-functional projects, and stretch assignments in coordination with the L&D COE. Tracking IDP progress and updating the succession bench accordingly.
Business review participation with people agenda ownership›
Serving as the primary intelligence channel from the BU to the CHRO. Surfacing ground-level workforce signals — cultural shifts, risks, compensation equity concerns, leadership capability gaps — before they escalate.
Shopfloor attrition patterns indicating BU-level systemic risk›
HRBP monitors and interprets shopfloor attrition patterns as a signal of BU-level systemic risk — advising HOD (IR) on intervention but not owning the resolution process.
Shopfloor cultural issues — advises HOD (IR) on intervention›
Where shopfloor cultural issues indicate BU-level risk, the HRBP advises HOD (IR) on the appropriate intervention. HRBP provides the strategic lens; HOD (IR) executes on the ground.
L&D programme design — HRBP provides skill gap data to COE›
HRBP owns training needs identification at BU level and surfaces skill gap data to the L&D COE. The COE owns programme architecture, content, and facilitators. HRBP does not design programmes.
Compensation benchmarking inputs — surfaces equity gaps›
HRBP surfaces compensation equity gaps within the BU for E and above grade employees. COE-C&B owns the benchmarking methodology and designs the correction mechanism. HRBP provides the BU context; COE provides the solution.
Cross-BU talent movements — recommends; CHRO and COE decide›
HRBP identifies cross-BU talent movement opportunities and recommends candidates. CHRO and COE own the decision and execution. HRBP provides the BU talent context but does not unilaterally move employees across units.
Policy exceptions — HRBP can request; COE approves or rejects›
Where business circumstances warrant a policy exception, the HRBP is authorised to formally request one. The COE holds approval authority. HRBP cannot grant exceptions unilaterally.
Vendor and agency management for TA — HRBP provides requirements›
HRBP provides BU-specific role requirements and quality feedback on candidates to COE-TA. COE manages vendor relationships, contracts, and SLAs. HRBP is the intelligence source; COE manages the relationship.
Org design advisory — HRBP advises; BU Head and CHRO approve›
HRBP advises the HR head on team structure, role design, and reporting relationships during growth, restructuring, or leadership change. Provides workforce impact analysis before structural decisions are finalised. BU Head and CHRO hold final approval authority.
Payroll processing, salary queries, PF/ESIC administration›
All payroll processing, salary query resolution, and PF/ESIC administration belongs to GCC (Shared Services). HRBP involvement in these activities is a misallocation of strategic capacity and must be redirected immediately.
Shopfloor attendance tracking and leave management›
Shopfloor attendance tracking and leave management for shopfloor grade employees belongs to HOD (IR) and Plant HR. HRBP is not involved in day-to-day attendance administration.
Letter issuance — GCC›
Experience letters, relieving letters, salary certificates, and all HR documentation issuance is handled by GCC. HRBP is informed of letters issued but does not generate or release them.
Shopfloor disciplinary proceedings and domestic enquiries — HOD (IR)›
All shopfloor disciplinary proceedings, domestic enquiry processes, show cause notices, and suspension decisions belong exclusively to HOD (IR) and the IR team. HRBP is informed of outcomes but does not participate in the process.
Union negotiations, collective bargaining, and labour law compliance›
All union interaction, collective bargaining, works committee negotiations, and labour law compliance belongs to HOD (IR). HRBP is informed of outcomes. Involvement beyond this boundary creates dual accountability and weakens IR management.
HRIS data entry and record maintenance — GCC›
All HRIS data entry, employee record maintenance, and system updates belong to GCC. HRBP consults and reviews data but does not maintain records. Asking an HRBP to update HRIS is a misuse of the role.
Contract labour documentation and statutory compliance — HOD (IR)›
Contract labour management, documentation, statutory compliance for contract workers, and Factory Act compliance belongs to HOD (IR). HRBP is not involved in contract labour administration.
Canteen, transport, and facility-related employee welfare — HOD (IR)›
All facility-related welfare activities — canteen management, transport arrangements, accommodation, and shopfloor welfare programmes — belong to HOD (IR). These are operationally important but outside the HRBP's remit.
1.4 · Key Responsibilities

Strategic and operational.
In the right ratio.

Responsibilities split into strategic and operational categories. The target ratio is 65% strategic to 35% operational — this governs weekly prioritisation decisions.

Strategic — 65%
Operational — 35%
Target weekly allocation
Strategic
Workforce Strategy & Planning
Lead annual headcount planning for the BU in partnership with the plant head and finance. Produce an evidence-based workforce plan accounting for attrition forecasts, skill gaps, and business growth targets. Present the BU people plan at the CHRO's annual review.
Strategic
Talent Health Management
Maintain a talent health data covering attrition risk by grade, high-potential status, succession bench coverage, and critical role vacancy age. Update monthly and present at BU business reviews.
Strategic
Succession Planning
Own succession bench mapping for all critical and senior roles (M and above). Conduct bi-annual 9-box calibration with the plant head. Ensure every critical role has at least one identified internal successor with a documented development plan.
Strategic
Performance Ecosystem Ownership
Facilitate the full PMS cycle for E and above grade employees — from KRA alignment through mid-year review to annual calibration. Ensure rating distributions are equitable and data-driven. Coach managers on structured developmental feedback.
Strategic
Manager Capability Development
Identify people management capability gaps among BU managers. Work with the L&D COE to nominate targeted interventions. Conduct individual coaching conversations on feedback, underperformance handling, and team trust.
Strategic
Engagement Diagnosis & Action
Analyse BU-level engagement and pulse survey data. Identify root causes rather than surface scores. Build and own a quarterly action plan with the HR head. Track progress at every monthly one-on-one.
Strategic
Organisational Design Advisory
Advise the HR head on team structure, role design, and reporting relationships during growth, restructuring, or leadership change. Provide workforce impact analysis before structural decisions are finalised.
Strategic
CHRO Intelligence Feed
Serve as the primary intelligence channel from the BU to the CHRO. Surface ground-level workforce signals — cultural shifts, risks, compensation equity concerns, leadership capability gaps — before they escalate.

These responsibilities are necessary but must be managed efficiently and should not displace strategic priorities. Where possible, delegate, systematise, or transition to the appropriate function.

Operational
E+ Grade Talent Acquisition Facilitation
Participate in shortlisting, interviewing, and offer approval for E and above grade roles. Review job descriptions for accuracy and business relevance. Manage business leader expectations on timelines and own candidate experience.
Operational
Onboarding Management
Facilitate Day 1 induction for new joiners (E and above grade). Own the 30/60/90-day check-in process. Monitor probation progress and conduct structured probation review conversations.
Operational
PMS Process Execution
Coordinate the PMS calendar for the BU — goal-setting submissions, mid-year review completion, and appraisal form submission. Track completion rates and escalate non-compliance to the plant head.
Operational
HiPo Programme Nominations
Identify and nominate HiPos for leadership programmes, cross-functional projects, and stretch assignments in coordination with the L&D COE. Track IDP progress and update the succession bench accordingly.
Operational
Exit Management
Conduct exit interviews for all E and above grade separations. Analyse themes quarterly and present aggregated insights to the plant head and CHRO. Lead retention conversations for at-risk high performers before resignation.
Operational
Monthly Reporting
Produce and share the BU people dashboard monthly, covering the 10 HRBP KPIs defined in the operating rhythm part of this playbook. This report is shared with both the BU Head HR and the CHRO.
Operational
Stakeholder Coordination
Coordinate with COE leads (TA, L&D, C&B) on BU-specific programme inputs, nominations, and data requests. Ensure no programme reaches the BU without prior HRBP context-setting.

No ambiguity.
